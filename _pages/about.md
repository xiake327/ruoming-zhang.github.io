I am a Ph.D. candidate in Economics at the [University of Delaware](https://www.udel.edu/), expected to graduate in August 2026. My research fields are **applied microeconomics**, **development economics**, **public policy**, **economics of education**, and **demographic economics**.

My work investigates how large-scale policy reforms in China shape human capital accumulation, marriage and fertility decisions, and labor market outcomes, with a particular focus on rural--urban disparities. I combine microdata from China's population censuses and sample surveys with quasi-experimental research designs, including difference-in-differences, regression discontinuity, and event-study frameworks.

My job market paper, published in the *Journal of Development Economics*, examines how China's 1999 higher education expansion propagated upstream to upper-secondary schooling and downstream to labor allocation, with differential effects by rural status.

**Research Fields:** Applied Microeconomics, Development Economics, Public Policy, Economics of Education, Demographic Economics
