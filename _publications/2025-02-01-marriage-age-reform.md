**Working Paper**

Using the 1981 nationwide abolition of administrative late-marriage rules and a regression-kink design with the 2010 China 1% Census, I find that delaying first marriage by one year lowers completed fertility by about 0.20 births, with stronger effects among urban and more-educated women.
